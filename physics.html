The Physics of Noise Colors

In audio engineering, "colored" noise refers to signals where the power spectral density (PSD) follows a power-law relationship:

S(f) ∝ 1 / f α

White Noise (α = 0)

White noise has a flat PSD. It contains equal power per unit of bandwidth across the spectrum.

Pink Noise (α = 1)

Pink noise, or 1/f noise, has equal power per octave. The power density decreases by 3 dB per octave.

Brownian Noise (α = 2)

Brownian noise (Red noise) decreases by 6 dB per octave. It is mathematically the integral of white noise.
